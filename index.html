About

From Worker Engagement
to Governance Analysis

My professional foundation is rooted in worker engagement. Over time, that experience reshaped how I understand labour exploitation and corporate accountability.

Individual cases reveal patterns. Patterns expose systemic weaknesses. Systemic weaknesses point to regulatory design. This progression moved my focus from responding to grievances toward examining the legal and governance architectures that shape corporate behaviour across borders.

Having experienced migration firsthand, I understand that governance is not abstract. Access to remedy, clear contracts, ethical recruitment, and safe reporting channels determine whether work leads to stability or harm. For me, supply chain governance is about ensuring that dignity is structurally protected within global trade systems.

Career Direction

My primary direction is to become a Human Rights Due Diligence Specialist within multinational corporations and international organisations — advancing accountable labour governance in global supply chains by embedding worker voice into compliance systems and regulatory frameworks.
